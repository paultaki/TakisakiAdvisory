Fixer File #002

The Quota Rebellion

They told me quotas couldn’t be fixed. So I built a new system—and it went national.

🕵️ INTEL BRIEF:

  • 🗺 Region: 7-State Territory
  • 🎯 Target: B2B Quota System
  • 👥 Org: Hundreds of SMB reps + regional managers
  • 💹 Initial State: Broken comp, top talent leaving, no trust
  • 💨 Final Result: National model adopted across Verizon B2B

🔍 THE DIAGNOSIS:

The quota model was failing—quietly, consistently, and culturally.
High performers were punished. Low performers were rewarded.
Leaders burned time monthly arguing fairness.
Reps had no visibility. Trust in leadership and Finance? Zero.

“I couldn’t hold anyone accountable. Quotas weren’t tracking potential—they were tracking past chaos.”

⚙️ WHAT I DID:

Built a 4D Quota Model:

  • Territory Potential (40%) – ranked by maturity, growth, opportunity
  • Zip Code Complexity (10%) – credited reps in rural grind zones
  • Active Customer Base (30%) – rewarded upsell/cross-sell leverage
  • Performance Influence (20%) – past results mattered, but didn’t dominate

“This wasn’t just math. It was behavioral design.”

Messaged It Like a Movement:

  • Org-wide kickoffs + live Q&A
  • Team workshops with territory breakdowns
  • Peer-led quota validation exercises
  • Total transparency: payout averages, zone logic, role-specific insights

💥 THE IMPACT:

  • 💬 Quota complaints: 0 for a full quarter
  • 📊 19 months of record-breaking YOY growth
  • 🤸 Top reps asked for high-quota territories
  • 🏆 Zone 1 became a badge of honor, not a burden
  • 📦 Reps retained, bottom 20% replaced
  • 🪜 Became national B2B model at Verizon
“Six years later, it’s still the backbone of Verizon’s B2B performance system.”

🧠 WHAT I LEARNED:

  • Fair ≠ Equal. Honor reality, not flat formulas.
  • You can’t fix behavior with pep talks. Fix the structure.
  • Math can motivate—if the story is clear.
