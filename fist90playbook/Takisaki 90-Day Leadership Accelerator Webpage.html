Overview

The first 90 days are your proving ground. Built from Paul Takisaki’s 20-year Fortune 50 playbook, this tool empowers you to inspire trust, secure buy-in, and set a transformative trajectory. Centered on strength-based mapping, it unlocks team potential, proven in turnarounds: 27% growth in the Pacific Northwest, 37% revenue in the Desert Team. Executable in 10 hours/week.

"Unlock your team’s hidden power in 90 days." – Paul Takisaki

Core Principles

  • Immersion: Uncover truth and strengths. "Never trust the first version."
  • Visibility: Show up where work happens. "I sit where conversations start."
  • Early Wins: Fix pain points fast. "Fix it in 30 days, they’ll follow."
  • Strengths Focus: Amplify talents. "Unleash what’s world-class."
  • Prioritization: Focus on vital metrics. "Win the must-haves."

Quick Start Guide

Time: 10 hours/week for 90 days.

  • Prepare (1 hr): Review, download templates.
  • Weeks 1–2 (5 hrs): Share story, 1:1s, map strengths.
  • Weeks 3–4 (5 hrs): Diagnose, meet customers, select metrics.
  • Weeks 5–8 (5 hrs): Implement wins, start calibration calls.
  • Weeks 9–12 (5 hrs): Communicate vision, track progress.
  • Track: Use Strength Mapping Tracker, Vital Few Scorecard.
  • Feedback: See Feedback section below.

Phase 1: Human Introduction (Weeks 1–2)

Objective: Build trust through vulnerability, strengths.

Task: Authentic Introduction

Why: Teams follow stories.

  • Share struggle (e.g., mall kiosk) with photos.
  • Admit limits: “I’m here for your strengths.”
  • Express: “I joined to build on your talents.”

Script: “I’m Paul, a father who started door-to-door, doubting I’d make it. Leadership is unlocking your strengths. What’s your story?”

Task: Frontline 1:1 Immersion

Why: Uncover talents.

  • Small Teams: Meet all (20-min).
  • Large Teams: Stratified (25% each: high-potentials, random, problem areas, self-nominated).
  • Ask (use 1:1 Conversation Script):
    • What energizes you? (Strengths)
    • What are you best at? (Strengths)
    • What would you do more? (Strengths)
    • What’s working?
    • What would you change?
    • What’s your approach? (Reflective)
  • Thank: “Your strengths shape our win.”

Phase 2: Intelligence Gathering (Weeks 3–4)

Objective: Diagnose, map strengths, prioritize metrics.

Task: Diagnose Territory

Why: Tailor moves.

  • Assess: Turnaround, Growth, Stability, Startup.
  • Tools: 12–24 months data, 1:1 strengths, “Will I get fired?” for 3–5 metrics.

Task: Meet 10–20 Customers

Why: Align insights.

  • Ask: What’s working? Change?
  • Debrief: “What’s one change?”

Task: Engage Influencers

Why: Amplify vision.

  • Identify via 1:1s.
  • Ask: “What am I missing?”

Task: Triangulate Truth

Why: Align priorities.

  • Compare: “Reporting frustrates reps. Your take?”

Phase 3: Early Win (Weeks 5–8)

Objective: Deliver strength-based wins.

Task: Secure Visible Wins

Why: Prove strengths.

  • Pick 2–3: Solvable, impactful.
  • Prioritize: Realignments, simplification.
  • Announce: “You said X, Y’s strength fixed it.”

Task: Hero Request

Why: Solve “no.”

  • Find request, pitch gatekeepers.

Task: Pilot Calibration Calls

Why: Share wins.

  • 30-min: Highlights, “One insight?”

Phase 4: Strategic Direction (Weeks 9–12)

Objective: Inspire vision.

Task: Develop and Communicate Vision

Why: Root in strengths.

  • Five Questions: Aspiration, Play, Win, Capabilities, Systems.
  • Vital Few Scorecard: “3 metrics.”
  • 7 ways: All-hands, 1:1s, map, email, huddles, stories, doc.
  • 3 notes: “Your strength shaped this.”

Task: Establish Implementation

Why: Ownership.

  • Strength-based roles.
  • Check-ins, track metrics.

Task: All-Hands Reveal

Why: Show strengths.

  • Quote: “One said, ‘I’m best at Z.’ Realigned.”
  • End: “Our plan, your strengths.”

Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Overload: Focus strengths.
  • Rushing: Map first.
  • Defensiveness: Ask: “Your take?”
  • Overpromising: Be clear.
  • Ignoring Strengths: Realign.

Success Metrics

  • 80%+ 1:1 completion.
  • 10–20 customer meetings.
  • 2–3 wins (1+ strength-based).
  • Vision, 70%+ clarity.
  • 3–5 influencers.
  • Metrics tracked.
  • 1+ realignment.

For Large Organizations

  • Scale 1:1s: Quarterly rotation.
  • Travel: Desks, lunches.
  • Visible: Videos, AMAs.
  • Track: Strength maps.
