Overview

The first 90 days aren’t a transition—they’re your shot to prove you’re the leader who gets it. Forged from 20 years of Fortune 50 turnarounds, this interactive tool equips executives to inspire trust, secure buy-in, and set a transformative trajectory. It prioritizes strength-based mapping to unlock team potential, paired with focused execution, tailored for Fortune 500 leaders.

"Unlock your team’s hidden power in 90 days." – Paul Takisaki

Core Principles

  • Immersion: Connect directly to uncover truth and strengths. "Never trust the first version of the truth."
  • Visibility: Show up where work happens. "I sit where conversations start."
  • Early Wins: Fix pain points fast. "Fix it in 30 days, and they’ll follow you."
  • Strengths Focus: Amplify talents, not weaknesses. "Unleash what’s world-class."
  • Prioritization: Focus on vital metrics. "Win the must-haves, I’ll handle the rest."

Phase 1: Human Introduction (Weeks 1–2)

Objective: Build trust through vulnerability and strength discovery.

Task: Authentic Introduction

Why: Teams follow stories. Share yours to value their strengths.

  • Share a struggle (e.g., starting at a mall kiosk) with photos.
  • Admit limits: “I’m here to amplify your strengths.”
  • Express excitement: “I joined to build on your talents.”

Example Script: “I’m Paul, a father of three who started selling phones door-to-door, doubting I’d make it. Leadership is about unlocking your best work. What’s your story?”

Task: Frontline 1:1 Immersion

Why: Uncover truths and talents to transform teams.

  • Small Teams (<100): Meet all (20-min sessions).
  • Large Teams (100+): Stratified selection (25% high-potentials, random, problem areas, self-nominated).
  • Ask:
    • What energizes you most? (Strengths)
    • What are you better at than anyone? (Strengths)
    • If you could redesign your role, what would you do more? (Strengths)
    • What’s working well?
    • What would you change?
    • What do you think is the best approach? (Reflective)
  • Thank sincerely: “Your strengths shape our win.”

Phase 2: Intelligence Gathering (Weeks 3–4)

Objective: Diagnose, map strengths, and prioritize metrics.

Task: Diagnose Territory

Why: Tailor moves to team needs, leveraging strengths.

  • Assess: Turnaround (low morale), Growth (plateaued), Stability (inefficiencies), Startup (chaos).
  • Tools: Review 12–24 months data, combine with 1:1 strengths, apply “Will I get fired?” test for 3–5 vital metrics (e.g., engagement).

Task: Meet 10–20 Customers

Why: External insights align with strengths.

  • Observe calls, ask: What’s working? What would you change?
  • Debrief with reflective questions: “What’s one change you’d prioritize?”

Task: Engage Influencers

Why: Amplify vision via their strengths.

  • Identify via 1:1s, ask: “What am I missing?”
  • Position as win leaders (e.g., storyteller for communication).

Task: Triangulate Truth

Why: Align strengths and priorities.

  • Compare 1:1s with leadership: “Reporting frustrates reps. What’s your take?”

Phase 3: Early Win (Weeks 5–8)

Objective: Deliver strength-based wins, simplify processes.

Task: Secure Visible Wins

Why: Prove you value strengths.

  • Pick 2–3 wins: Solvable in 30 days, impactful, tied to feedback.
  • Prioritize:
    • Role realignments (e.g., rep to client engagement).
    • Simplify processes (e.g., consolidate reports).
    • Kill approvals, fix tools.
  • Announce: “You said X, Y’s strength fixed it.”

Task: Hero Request

Why: Solve a “no” to show strength focus.

  • Find denied request (e.g., role shift).
  • Pitch gatekeepers: “One win to show we value strengths.”

Task: Pilot Calibration Calls

Why: Share wins, build ownership.

  • Weekly 30-min calls: Highlights, opportunities, “One peer insight?”
  • Ask: “How can [win] amplify your strengths?”

Phase 4: Strategic Direction (Weeks 9–12)

Objective: Inspire a strength-driven vision, operationalize focus.

Task: Develop and Communicate Vision

Why: Root “why” in strengths.

  • Use Five Questions: Aspiration, Play, Win, Capabilities, Systems.
  • Highlight Vital Few Scorecard: “These 3 metrics, your strengths get us there.”
  • Communicate 7 ways: All-hands, 1:1s, visual map, email, huddles, stories, “Why” doc.
  • Send 3 handwritten notes: “Your strength shaped our path.”

Task: Establish Implementation

Why: Vision needs strength-based ownership.

  • Set roles tied to strengths.
  • Weekly check-ins, track vital metrics.
  • AMAs, surveys: “How do your strengths contribute?”

Task: All-Hands Reveal

Why: Show strengths shape strategy.

  • Share themes: “You said X.”
  • Quote: “One said, ‘I’m best at Z.’ We realigned.”
  • Outline: “3 metrics, driven by talents.”
  • End: “Our plan, your strengths.”

Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Overload: Focus on strengths, vital metrics.
  • Rushing: Map strengths first.
  • Defensiveness: Ask: “What’s your take?”
  • Overpromising: Be clear on limits.
  • Ignoring Strengths: Realign, don’t fix weaknesses.

Success Metrics

  • 80%+ 1:1 completion.
  • 10–20 customer meetings.
  • 2–3 wins (1+ strength-based).
  • Vision communicated, 70%+ clarity.
  • 3–5 influencer allies.
  • Vital metrics tracked.
  • 1+ role realigned.

For Large Organizations

  • Scale 1:1s: Quarterly rotation (25% high-potentials, random, problem areas, self-nominated).
  • Travel: Work from desks, join lunches.
  • Stay Visible: Videos, AMAs, strength surveys.
  • Track: Share strength maps quarterly.

Case Study: Strength-Based Turnaround

In the Pacific Northwest, a dysfunctional team missed targets for seven quarters. Instead of firings, I mapped strengths via 1:1s, realigned roles (e.g., marketing manager to competitive intelligence), and simplified processes. By Q2, we hit 27% above target, led nationally by Q3, and engagement reached top 10%. Teams said, “I’m doing what I’m good at,” proving strengths transform.
