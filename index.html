About Paul

Chess Player in a Checker's World

For 20 years at Verizon, I felt like an outlier. My approach to leadership wasn't just unconventional—it ran counter to the dominant culture. I built strategy and stayed focused while others put in conspicuous 80-hour weeks and managed through force of personality.

I focused on the vital few priorities rather than the trivial many. I empowered my team instead of micromanaging. I sat in cubicles, not corner offices—because that's where you hear what's really happening.

This approach made me an enigma. Executives couldn't quite figure out why my teams consistently outperformed others with less drama and burnout.
